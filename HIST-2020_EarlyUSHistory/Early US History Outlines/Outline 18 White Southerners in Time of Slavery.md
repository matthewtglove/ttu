## WHITE SOUTHERNERS IN THE TIME OF SLAVERY

## I. Social Characteristics of the Antebellum South

1. Isolated & Rural
2. Agricultural
3. Self-Sufficient
4. Homogeneous
5. Jeffersonian (Pro State-Rights)
6. Traditional

## II. Social Structure

1. **==Planters==** **(Owners of 20+ Slaves)**
	- Men-High Debt
	- Frequent Moves
	- Constant Wariness
	- Staples: Rice, Cotton, Tobacco
	- Women-Oversight of House Staff, Hospitality
	- Control of Account Books
	- Lived in Presence of Husband’s Slave Children
2. **Small Slaveowners (Owners of 1-19 Slaves)**
	- Merchants, Hotel Operators, Middle Class
	- Produced Staples for Market
3. **==Yeomen==** **(Non-Slaveowners)**
	- Independent Farmers, Lived in Hill & Mountain Areas
	- Self-Sufficient
	- Produced Grains and Meat
4. **==Poor Whites==** **(Squatters)**
	- Crude, Irregular Lives, Hap-Hazard Farming
	- Called “White Trash” by Whites and Blacks

## III. ==Pro-Slavery Ideology==

1. **The Link between Non-Slaveowners and the Plantation System**
	- Annual Cattle Drives
	- “Hiring Out” at Harvest Time
	- Fear of Job Competition
	- ==Night-time Patrols==
2. **==Slavery as a “Positive Good”==**
	- History
	- Religion
	- Economy
	- Science
	- Security
