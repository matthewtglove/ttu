## The Colonies’ “Growing Pains”

## I. The attempt to establish conformity in New England

1. ==Roger Williams==
	- Sympathy for the Indians
	- Providence, RI
2. ==Anne Hutchinson==
	- Challenges gender roles
	- Challenges the authority of the clergy
	- Antinomianism
3. The ==Salem Witch Hunt== (1692)
	- Puritan ministers try to stamp out the local practice of magic
	- Young servant girls accuse elderly women of witchcraft
	- Hysteria spreads throughout Massachusetts

## II. ==Bacon’s Rebellion== in Virginia (1676)

1. Indian raids in the backcountry
2. Dispute between the royal governor and the wealthy planters
3. Servants and yeoman farmers take matters into their own hands
4. Jamestown destroyed, and the decline of indentured servitude

## III. The broken promise of the Georgia colony

1. The idealism of ==James Oglethorpe==
2. The reality of conditions along the Savannah River
3. The influence of the Carolinians

## IV. ==Lord Calvert=='s Experiment

1. Persecution of Catholics in England
2. The Feudal State
3. Maryland's settlers refuse to obey

## V. ==The== ==Stono== ==Uprising== (1739)

1. Severe smallpox epidemic hits South Carolina
2. War breaks out between Britain and Spain
3. “Jemmy” leads a rebellion along the Stono River

## VI. ==The Great Awakening== (1739-1743)

1. Colonial expansion into the backcountry
2. Influence of the European Enlightenment
3. ==Jonathan Edwards== employs “fire and brimstone”
4. “==New Lights==” versus “==Old Lights==”
	- *NL - ==George Whitefield==*
	- *Presbyterians*
	- *Baptists*
	- *OL - Puritans (Congregationalists)*
