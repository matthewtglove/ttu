1 Week 3 Overview: Identity & Integrity
Your Week 2 implementation successfully solved the Key Distribution Problem using an Elliptic
Curve Diffie-Hellman (ECDH) exchange. However, it introduced a catastrophic new vulnerability:
an active Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) attack. Because your ephemeral X25519 public keys were
sent in plaintext without any proof of origin, an attacker could intercept the connection, establish
two separate shared secrets (one with Alice, one with Bob), and quietly decrypt and re-encrypt the
traffic in real time.
This week, you will close this vulnerability by introducing Identity. You will use long-term
RSA-2048 keys to digitally sign your ephemeral ECDH public points before transmission.
2 Cryptographic Implementation Goals
You must update your handshake logic to incorporate RSA digital signatures. To do this, you will
use the cryptography.hazmat library, specifically the asymmetric.padding.PSS module.
2.1 Phase A: Long-Term Key Generation
We have provided a utility script named generate keys.py. Run this script once on your host ma-
chine to generate alice private.pem, alice public.pem, bob private.pem, and bob public.pem.
Note: In a real-world scenario, public keys are distributed via Digital Certificates (X.509). For this
lab, we will simulate a trusted out-of-band exchange by simply loading the peer’s public key from
disk.
2.2 Phase B: The Authenticated Handshake
Modify your Week 2 Client and Server code to perform the following:
1. Load Identity: Load your own RSA private key and your peer’s RSA public key from the
.pem files.
1
2. Sign: After generating your ephemeral X25519 public key, use your RSA private key to
generate a digital signature of those public bytes.
3. Transmit: Send both the 256-byte signature and the 32-byte X25519 public key over the
socket.
4. Verify: When you receive your peer’s transmission, use their RSA public key to verify the
signature against the received X25519 bytes. If the verification fails, drop the connection
immediately (raise an exception).

3. The Deliverable: In your lab report, explain the timing vulnerability. Then, research and
